Lewis Caroll

A One-Hit Wonder

The strategy of our first literary alchemist is both the simplest and perhaps the most difficult to replicate. In 1865, Lewis Carroll wrote Alice's Adventures in Wonderland, a work that has since become a timeless classic. Over the years, it has been adapted into nearly 13 different films, spanning from 1915 to 2010. This remarkable success highlights the enduring power of Carroll's creation, demonstrating how a single work can continue to resonate with audiences and be reinterpreted across different generations. His secret ingredient ? The art of a blending whimsical yet refreshing absurdity with deeper philosophical ideas.

Agatha Christie

The Serial Writer

Our second alchemist's strategy is clever and captivating: Agatha Christie created the character Hercule Poirot and placed him at the center of a series of increasingly intricate adventures. Her secret lies in her mastery of intrigue, paired with the creation of an endearing character. This combination fosters a strong emotional connection with the audience, making it ideal for a series of adaptations, spin-offs, and episodic stories that keep viewers engaged. Murder and intrigue are the hallmarks of her work, be careful if you follow her leads, as they may send a chill down your spine!
